Boss-Up Summit

About

After our first conference, the Miss Boss Young Women Leadership Conference, where we brought together female leaders as speakers to inspire female students, we learnt that even our male friends who attended our conference to support us felt they got a lot from the speakers. It made us realize that the tools that we provide to our audience can be applicable to anybody.

We rebranded and changed our conference and organization name from “Miss Boss” to “Boss-Up”. Instead of focusing on women's leadership, Boss-Up is about leadership for everyone. We want youths everywhere to be able to take advantage of this resource and not be kept back from the marketing that says that our conference is geared towards woman only. Boss-Up is about being inclusive and welcoming towards everybody.

With this new rebranding in mind, we set out grand plans for Boss-Up Summit, but the pandemic happened. We had to shift from our original plans of renting out the Marriott Hotel Ballroom to going online.

We were frustrated at first, but we quickly realised that an online conference would be able to reach much bigger heights than an in-person event. Indeed, Boss-Up Summit consisted of speakers across North America including several Forbes 30 under 30 speakers. Boss-Up Summit ended as a tremendous success in which we had hundreds of youths come listen in to our amazing speakers and acquire new tools to navigate their adult life with.

Speakers

Kenneth Harris

Senior Engineer @ NASA Goddard Space Flight Center
Forbes 30 under 30 | Maryland

Kenneth Harris is a Senior Engineer at NASA Goddard Space Flight Center where he contributes his leadership in developing procedures and implementing processes between the Flight Team, Instrument Vendors, and Spacecraft Vendors. Named in Forbes 30 under 30 List of Innovators, Kenneth Harris has had an impressive resume of accomplishments despite his young age.

Mr. Harris served as the Deputy Lead Integration Engineer for the James Webb Space Telescope’s Integrated Science Instrument Module (ISIM). Mr. Harris led a team of 10 engineers to successfully integrate ISIM, the main payload of JWST, to the Backplane of the Optical Telescope Element (OTE) Primary Mirror. Not only does Kenneth Harris channel his expertise in the engineering field, he also personifies Boss-Up’s energy: helping youths.

Joseph Cohen

Founder of Universe
Forbes 30 under 30 | New York

Joseph Cohen, founder of Universe, a mobile app that provides website making services, walked an unconventional path, dropping out of Wharton and creating his own path. Universe is designed to provide its users with an easy, no-fuss website making process and has secured $10 million in Series A funding from Google Ventures.

While still a student at Wharton, he founded Lore, Inc. that raised $6 million in venture financing, and successfully sold the business in 2013. With an expertise in online tools and working on special projects, such as fashion brand, Reformation, Joseph Cohen is an experienced entrepreneur who has a diverse range of experience and accomplishments.

Susan Ho

Founder of Journy
Forbes 30 under 30 | New York

Travel+Leisure Magazine's "Best New Travel Apps of 2017"

With a passion for travelling and food, Susan Ho is the founder of Journy, an online service that pairs travelers with their own personal trip designers. Her platform was featured as one of Travel+Leisure Magazine's "Best New Travel Apps of 2017" while Susan Ho herself was named in the 2018 Forbes 30 Under 30 Consumer Tech list. As a UPenn Wharton graduate, she is an operations guru as she was Vice President of Operations Strategy and Customer Service at Fab.com, leading a team of over 120 across New York and Berlin.

She also has impressive consulting experience under her belt such as DigitalOcean, Blue Apron, and LearnVest. Her role as an Asian-American, representing the asian population, is essential to our emerging new generation.

May Boeve

Executive Director of 350.org
Time Magazine "Next Generation Leader" Honorable Mention
Elle Gold Award | Harvard's New Frontier Award

Bay Area

As the executive director of an international climate change campaign organization, 350.org. With 5,200 events in 181 countries and 350 rallies in places as far-flung as Kazakhstan and Ethiopia, 350 is a powerhouse in organizing young people to act against climate change.
